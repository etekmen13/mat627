\section{Problem Setup}
We solve
\[
T\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{b},
\]
where $T\in\mathbb{R}^{n\times n}$ is tridiagonal with
\[
t_{ij}=
\begin{cases}
1, & |i-j|=1,\\
i+1, & i=j,\\
0, & \text{otherwise,}
\end{cases}
\qquad
b_i=1.
\]
The implementation stores only the lower diagonal $\ell$, diagonal $d$, upper diagonal $u$, right-hand side $\mathbf{b}$, and solution $\mathbf{x}$, then applies Algorithm 2.6 (Thomas elimination and back substitution). After solving, it computes the residual
\[
\mathbf{r}=\mathbf{b}-T\mathbf{x}
\]
using tridiagonal matrix-vector multiplication and reports
\[
e_n=\max_{1\le i\le n}|r_i|.
\]
